Internship Experience

This past Summer, I worked at Hewlett-Packard Enterprise (HPE) as a Software Development Engineer Intern (SDE) under the High-Performance Computing and Artificial Intelligence Business unit. I was on the Chapel team which is a team consisting of ~20 people responsible for creating an open-source parallel programming language known as Chapel. You can learn more about Chapel here.

My task specifically was to work on the implementation of the “unstable” keyword in the language's modules and compiler. The "unstable" keyword (denoted as @unstable in the language) displays if a symbol in the Chapel language may misbehave or is subject to change in the future. This feature is mostly useful to the Chapel developers, as it allows them to better organize the language as they continue to develop it. This effort took 13 weeks and over 1000 lines of code for its design, tests, and implementation.
